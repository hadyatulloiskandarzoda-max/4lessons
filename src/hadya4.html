Panjakent: The Pompeii of Central Asia

Step back in time to the ancient Sogdian city of Panjakent, a vital crossroads on the Silk Road, renowned for its remarkably preserved ruins and vibrant frescoes.

Discover Panjakent's Legacy

Panjakent, meaning "Five Villages," was a thriving city-state in Sogdiana during the 5th to 8th centuries CE. Strategically located on the Zarafshan River, it became a crucial trading and cultural center along the Silk Road, connecting East and West. Its ruins offer an unparalleled glimpse into Sogdian civilization before the Arab conquest. First systematically excavated by Soviet archaeologists in the mid-20th century, Panjakent revealed a sophisticated urban layout, monumental temples, a royal palace, bustling marketplaces, and richly decorated residential quarters. The city's artistic legacy, particularly its vibrant frescoes, provides invaluable insights into the religious beliefs, daily life, and mythology of the Sogdians.
